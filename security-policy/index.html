Security disclosure policy

Reporting a security concern

If you've found a vulnerability, data exposure, or security concern related to ReclaimShield™ or its appeal-recovery pipelines, we want to hear from you. This page tells you what's in scope, how to report, and what we commit to.

Machine-readable version of this policy lives at /.well-known/security.txt per RFC 9116.

How to report

Use the contact form on this site to file a report. Do not email findings to staff directly — the contact form ensures the report routes to the right queue.

Open the contact form → View security.txt

What to include

  • URL or endpoint affected
  • Reproduction steps, if any
  • Your assessment of severity (low / medium / high / critical)
  • Whether you intend to publicly disclose, and on what timeline

What's in scope

Anything ReclaimShield operates directly:

  • This site (reclaimshield.io)
  • The /meta and /amazon intake flows
  • The ReclaimShield Meta pipeline (AS-1, META-INTAKE-WEB, AS-3, AS-4)
  • The ReclaimShield Amazon pipeline (AS-1, AMAZON-INTAKE-WEB, AZ-3)
  • The contact, support-chat, verify, meta-intake, or amazon-intake Cloudflare Pages Functions
  • The Stripe payment flows
  • Any ReclaimShield deliverable email or letter

What's out of scope

  • Rate-limit / DDoS reports against the public site — use your provider's process
  • Reports against vendor services (Cloudflare, AWS, Postmark, Airtable, Make, Stripe, Anthropic, GitHub, etc.) — please report directly to those vendors
  • Findings against Meta, Instagram, Threads, or Amazon themselves — those go to their respective bug bounty programs, not us
  • Findings that depend on social engineering of ReclaimShield personnel

Our commitment to you

If you file a valid report through the contact form, we commit to:

  • Acknowledge your report within 72 hours
  • Confirm or refute the issue within 14 calendar days
  • Coordinate a remediation timeline with you before any public disclosure

Reports are reviewed by Sentinel Risk Group LLC personnel under the same disciplined process used to operate the appeal pipelines themselves.
