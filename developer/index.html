Apple

Apple App Store — Review Guidelines

Apple’s account-level enforcement mechanism. “Connected to a previously terminated account” without disclosure of which one, fraud-flag false positives (gift-card patterns, VPN-during-signup, refund-rate threshold trips), or multi-account violations. Apple’s analog to Google’s Identity Association. The Complex kit includes a Developer Code of Conduct Response Letter — an affidavit-style narrative documenting legitimate identity, business banking, and the connection-flag refutation, formatted for the App Review Board.
Original-app issues, impersonation, branding without approval, design lifted from another app. The reviewer is judging whether your app is a derivative work or an original. The Complex kit includes an Original-App Authentication Letter documenting your prior creation with code-commit timestamps, earlier-version screenshots, and third-party citations referencing your work — addressed to the App Review Board.
Third-party material without permission, false endorsements, IP-holder disputes. Two-track approach: a Rights-Holder Retraction Letter drafted on RS letterhead and sent by you to the complainant, and a Resolution Center submission addressed to Apple demonstrating the complaint is resolved or inaccurate. The platform usually defers to the rights-holder. When the rights-holder withdraws, Apple follows.
Placeholder text, non-functional URLs, crashes, bugs at first launch, missing demo accounts for review. The reviewer was unable to evaluate the app as a finished product. Resolution Center submission documents the specific completeness issue, the build correction made (or new build queued), and the reviewer-access information that was missing.
Misleading descriptions, hidden features, incomplete IAP descriptions, inaccurate age ratings. Apple’s reviewer compares your metadata against actual app behavior; mismatches trigger this category. Resolution Center submission addresses the specific metadata field flagged and documents the corrected text, screenshot, age rating, or IAP description.
IAP requirement violations, alternative-payment-mechanism rejections, subscription grace-period issues. Apple is aggressive on payments — redirecting users out of Apple’s IAP system, or failing to use IAP for unlock-features, lands here. Resolution Center submission documents the IAP routing change made and the affected feature flow going forward.
Repackaged websites, lack of utility or lasting value, low-effort wrappers. Apple wants apps that justify their place on the device. Resolution Center submission addresses the specific functionality the reviewer cited as missing and documents the new build’s expanded functionality.
Multiple Bundle IDs for similar apps, saturated-category variations, repeat submissions of essentially the same app. Apple treats this as developer-level enforcement, not just an individual-app rejection. Resolution Center submission documents the distinguishing functionality of the app or the consolidation plan where multiple are flagged.
Content that can be remediated by editing — offensive material removal, age-rating correction, sensitive-content gating. Different from Guidelines 1.2 / 1.4 / 1.6, which Apple treats as non-remediable and which we don’t cover. Resolution Center submission documents what specific content was flagged and the build changes made to satisfy the guideline.
Performance issues, hardware-specific bugs, incompatibility with current iOS / iPadOS, freezing on specific device classes. Resolution Center submission addresses the specific device or iOS version where the issue surfaced, the root-cause diagnosis, and the new build with the compatibility fix.
Google Play

Google Play — Developer Program Policies

Account-level association detection: device fingerprint, IP subnet, payment method, or phone number flagged as connecting your account to a previously terminated developer. Google’s #1 false-positive trigger in 2026 per its own enforcement reporting. Common scenarios: shared coworking IP, family-shared payment method, prior-employer device fingerprint, ISP-reassigned phone number. The Complex kit includes an Identity Association Response Letter — an affidavit-style narrative documenting the legitimate-or-no-connection story with the explicit request for manual review.
Trademark, copyright, or counterfeit-app accusations from a rights-holder. Two-track approach: a Rights-Holder Retraction Letter drafted on RS letterhead and sent by you to the complaining party, and a Plan of Action addressed to Google explaining why the complaint should be withdrawn or is inaccurate.
The Android Developer Verification framework, with enforcement beginning September 30, 2026 in Brazil, Indonesia, Singapore, and Thailand and expanding globally through 2027. Verification requires legal name, address, email, and phone for individuals; D-U-N-S number, organization-website verification, and possibly government-ID upload for organizations. The Complex kit includes a Verification Response Letter drafted with placeholders — you fill them in from your own records before submitting to Google. We never collect, store, or transmit verification documentation.
Misleading metadata, fake claims, hidden functionality, impersonation. The reviewer flags listings where what the user sees in the Store doesn’t match what the app does — hidden capabilities, false advertising claims, business names implying a relationship that doesn’t exist. Plan of Action ties the deception flag to the specific listing field or in-app behavior and documents the corrective change.
Multiple package names for similar apps, low-effort or repackaged content, saturated-category variants. POA documents what was actually published, why each app is distinguishable, and the consolidation plan if multiple are flagged together.
Adult content, gambling, alcohol / tobacco / cannabis policy violations, dangerous-products listings. Categories where remediation is contractually permitted but the listing must satisfy specific gating, age-rating, and regional-availability rules. POA addresses which restricted-content rule was breached and how the listing has been corrected.
Data-collection-without-disclosure, missing or non-compliant privacy policy, SDK privacy violations, Data Safety section breaches. POA documents the data the app actually collects, the disclosure that has been corrected, and the compliance plan going forward.
Misuse of high-risk permissions: SMS, Call Log, Accessibility Service, all-files access, background location. Google publishes use-case-specific gating for each high-risk permission. POA documents the permission’s legitimate function, removes uses that don’t fit, and demonstrates remaining usage matches an approved use case.
Disruptive ads, deceptive ad placements, in-app-purchase circumvention, off-Google-Play payment redirection. POA addresses the specific monetization element flagged and documents the corrective placement, disclosure, or IAP routing change.
Misleading screenshots, exaggerated functionality claims, prohibited promotional language, manipulative calls-to-action in metadata. The Store-listing page is policed separately from in-app behavior. POA addresses the specific listing element flagged and documents the corrective change.
Why our kit wins

Seven things baked into the methodology that average DIY doesn’t have.

None of these are claims about reverse-engineering platform AI. They’re about how an attorney-designed methodology, built from a careful read of every published policy section, gives you a structurally better submission than a Fiverr template or a self-drafted letter.

The reviewer panel literally scans for the section your notice cites. A Plan of Action that doesn’t address Section 4.1 / 5.6 / Identity Association directly fails on the first read — regardless of writing quality. Section-citation discipline is the methodology floor.

Reserve Escalation Letter included

Most providers deliver only the Plan of Action. Our kit always includes the Senior Reviewer or App Review Board Escalation Letter pre-drafted, held back unless the first appeal is denied. Most appeals that eventually reinstate do so at this step — not at the first POA.

Built from a direct read of every published policy

An attorney with platform-enforcement experience read every Apple App Store Review Guideline section and every Google Play Developer Program Policy category, then designed the kit around them. Not a translated Amazon Seller POA.

Two-track IP approach

For trademark and copyright cases, we draft both a Rights-Holder Retraction Letter (you send it to the complainant) and the platform-facing appeal, designed to run in parallel. The platform usually defers to the rights-holder. When the rights-holder withdraws, the platform follows.

We never ask for your credentials

Apple Developer Program License Agreement § 2.1 specifically prohibits sharing developer-account access. Many Fiverr operators ask for your Console login. Our refusal isn’t a courtesy — it’s contractually correct. You file from your own session, in your own voice, under your own name.

Bi-weekly platform-change monitoring

We track Google’s September 30, 2026 Android Developer Verification framework rollout on a bi-weekly schedule — new countries added, framework changes, document-requirement shifts. The kit stays current with what the platform is actually doing.

Pre-flight refund on unappealable cases

For EU and EEA Developers

Same kit and pricing. Different forum if a dispute proceeds past the platform.

Schedule APP applies to developers worldwide per § APP.5. The Digital Services Act gives EU and EEA developers additional statutory rights: Article 17 (reasoned statement of reasons), Article 20 (internal complaint-handling reviewed by qualified personnel — not solely automation), and Article 21 (referral to certified out-of-court dispute-settlement bodies, where the developer-platform Article 21 ecosystem is still maturing).

If a dispute proceeds past the platform’s internal channels: Apple DPLA § 14.10(d) routes EU and EEA developer matters to the exclusive jurisdiction of Irish courts under Irish law. Google DDA § 16.8 applies California law and Santa Clara County jurisdiction uniformly worldwide — no EU split in Google’s contract. Our kit’s deliverables can support the exercise of DSA rights but are not Article 21 dispute-settlement-body submissions and don’t substitute for representation before any EU regulator. Developers seeking a fully DSA-tailored deliverable should consult counsel familiar with EU platform-regulation practice.
